7
1 My son, keep my words
and store up my commands within you.
2 Keep my commands and live
and keep my instruction as the apple of your eye.
3 Tie them on your fingers;
write them on the tablet of your heart.
4 Say to wisdom, "You are my sister,"
and call understanding your kinsman,
5 in order to keep you from the seductive woman,
from the adulteress with her smooth words.
6 At the window of my house
I looked out through the lattice
7 and I saw many young men who had not been taught.
I saw among the youths
a young man who had no sense.
8 That young man passed through the street near her corner
and he went toward her house—
9 it was twilight, in the evening of the day,
at the time of the night and darkness.
10 And there a woman met him,
dressed like a prostitute, and she knew why she was there.
11 She is loud and wayward,
her feet do not stay at home—
12 now in the streets, now in the market,
at every corner she lies in wait.
13 So she grabs him and kisses him,
with a bold face she says to him,
14 "I made my peace offering today,
I paid my vows,
15 so came I out to meet you,
to eagerly seek your face, and I have found you.
16 I have spread coverings on my bed,
colored linens from Egypt.
17 I have perfumed my bed
with myrrh, aloes, and cinnamon.
18 Come, let us take our fill of love till morning;
let us take great pleasure in different acts of love-making.
19 My husband is not at home;
he is away on a long journey.
20 He took a bag of money with him;
he will return on the day of the full moon."
21 With her seductive talk she persuades him,
and with her smooth talk she compels him.
22 He walks after her
like an ox going to the slaughter,
or like a deer caught in a trap
23 until an arrow pierces through its liver—
or like bird rushing into a snare,
not knowing it will cost him his life.
24 And now, my sons, listen to me;
pay attention to what I say.
25 Do not let your heart turn aside to her ways;
do not go astray into her paths.
26 She has brought down many victims;
they cannot be counted.
27 Her house is on the road to sheol;
it goes down to the rooms of death.
